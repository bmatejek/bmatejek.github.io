Hi, I am Brian Matejek. I recently defended my Ph.D., researching biologically-aware algorithms for connectomics in the Visual Computing Group at Harvard University, working under the guidance of Hanspeter Pfister. Within connectomics, I focused on scalability problems as the datasets exceeded petabytes in size. Specifically, I investigated: compression techniques for the raw image data and corresponding label volumes, error correction methods to improve current state-of-the-art segmentation strategies, topological thinning algorithms for simplifying the label volumes, and subgraph enumeration techniques for identifying small structural elements in the extracted wiring diagram. Before pursuing my Ph.D., I received a B.S.E. and M.S.E. from Princeton University, working primarily with Thomas Funkhouser on computer vision applications.
